# INTERVIEW PREP: Mock Backward Design Session Script
**Scenario:** "Phi, walk us through how you would design a training program for [Topic X] from scratch."
**Format:** 15-minute high-impact delivery.

---

## 0–3 MIN: THE "DIAGNOSTIC" OPENING
> *Goal: Stop thinking like a teacher, start thinking like a Business Partner.*

"Before I touch any design tool, I start with the **Business Problem**. I'd ask stakeholders:
1. What is the **Performance Gap**? Is this a lack of knowledge, a lack of skill, or an environmental blocker?
2. What does **Success** look like in measurable terms? (e.g., 20% faster resolution time, 15% fewer errors).
3. If we do nothing, what is the **Cost of Inaction**?

Once the 'Why' is crystal clear, I apply the **Backward Design** framework."

---

## 3–7 MIN: STAGE 1 — IDENTIFY DESIRED RESULTS
"I define the **Learning Outcomes** first. I don't ask what I want to *teach*; I ask what the learner must be able to *do* independently after the course.
- I use **Bloom's Taxonomy** but focus on the 'Application' and 'Synthesis' levels.
- Example: Not 'Identify 3 parts of an email', but 'Write an email that secures a client meeting'."

---

## 7–11 MIN: STAGE 2 — DETERMINE ASSESSMENT EVIDENCE
"This is where many designers miss the mark. I design the **Test** before the content.
- How will we know they got it? 
- If the goal is a behavioral shift, a multiple-choice quiz isn't enough. I'd design a **Branching Scenario** where they face a trade-off. 
- The assessment must mirror the actual job environment. If it's a software training, the assessment is a task in a sandbox environment."

---

## 11–14 MIN: STAGE 3 — PLAN LEARNING EXPERIENCES
"Only now do I plan the content. I follow the **Gagne’s 9 Events** or **ARCS Model** to ensure engagement.
- **Hook them:** Start with a real-world crisis or 'What if' scenario (like my *Deadline Crisis* project).
- **Instruction:** Keep it modular. 5-minute bursts. 
- **Active Practice:** Every 10 minutes of intake requires 5 minutes of output/practice."

---

## 14–15 MIN: THE "TECH-ARCHITECT" CLOSER
"Finally, I consider the **Deployment Strategy**.
- Which LMS? 
- SCORM for completion or xAPI for behavioral tracking?
- How will we iterate? I’ll look at the data after the first 50 learners to see where they struggle and refine the content.

**That is how I ensure we don't just 'train' people, but actually solve the business problem.**"

---

## KEY PHRASES TO DROP (TITANIUM ATTRIBUTES)
- *"Instructional design is project management for the mind."*
- *"We are moving from 'Information Push' to 'Performance Pull'."*
- *"I design for the learner's cognitive load, not for the SME's ego."*
- *"Data tells me where the design failed; the learner tells me why."*
