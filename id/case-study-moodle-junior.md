# IELTS Writing Task 2 Course — Criteria-Based Assessment on Moodle

## The Problem

Most IELTS Writing courses tell learners their band score without explaining why they received it. A learner who gets 6.0 on Task Achievement often doesn't know which specific aspect of their response caused the deduction — was it the position statement, the supporting ideas, or the conclusion?

This project was designed around one question: how do we give learners enough transparency about evaluation criteria that they can self-assess and improve between sessions?

## Core Objective

Build a 4-unit IELTS Writing Task 2 course on Moodle with:
- Assessment criteria mapped directly to official IELTS Band Descriptors
- A 4-level rubric that learners can reference when reading teacher feedback
- Completion tracking and activity gating to ensure sequential progression through units
- A peer forum for learners to discuss and compare responses

## What I Built

A self-paced IELTS Writing Task 2 course on MoodleCloud with:

- **4 units** covering: Understanding the Task → Essay Structure → Writing the Essay → Assessment & Feedback
- **4-level rubric** per band score mapped directly to official IELTS Band Descriptors across 4 criteria: Task Achievement, Coherence & Cohesion, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range & Accuracy
- **Activity gating** — learners cannot access Unit 2 until Unit 1 is marked complete, enforcing a structured learning path
- **MCQ knowledge checks** at the end of each unit
- **Peer forum** for learners to submit and compare practice essays
- **Completion tracking** visible to the course administrator

## Design Decisions

**Why use official Band Descriptors instead of simplified criteria?**
Simplifying the criteria creates a gap between what learners study and what examiners actually use. Mapping directly to the official descriptors means learners are practicing with the same language their work will be judged against.

**Why a 4-level rubric instead of a single score?**
A single band score tells a learner where they are. A rubric with 4 criteria tells them why — and which specific area to prioritize in their next practice session.

**Why activity gating?**
Without gating, learners skip to the final assessment and miss foundational concepts. Gating enforces the pedagogical sequence without requiring instructor intervention.

## What I Learned

- Moodle's rubric tool is powerful but requires careful setup. Criteria need to be written in learner-friendly language — not examiner language — while still mapping accurately to the source descriptors.
- Completion tracking and activity gating taught me how LMS structure itself can guide learner behavior without instructor intervention at every step.
- Building the peer forum showed me that social learning features need clear instructions — without guidance on what a useful peer response looks like, learners default to "Good essay!" comments.

## Tools Used

Moodle 4.x (MoodleCloud) · IELTS Band Descriptors (official source) · MCQ assessments · Rubric-based grading · Activity completion tracking
