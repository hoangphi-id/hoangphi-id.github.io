Case Study 2

IELTS Writing Task 2 Course — Criteria-Based Assessment on Moodle

Designing criteria-based assessment for IELTS learners on Moodle.

Moodle 4.x IELTS Band Descriptors Rubric Logic Activity Gating Completion Tracking

The Problem

Most IELTS Writing courses tell learners their band score without explaining why they received it. A learner who gets 6.0 on Task Achievement often doesn't know which specific aspect of their response caused the deduction — was it the position statement, the supporting ideas, or the conclusion?

This project was designed around one question: how do we give learners enough transparency about evaluation criteria that they can self-assess and improve between sessions?

Core Objective

Build a 4-unit IELTS Writing Task 2 course on Moodle that maps directly to official Band Descriptors, ensuring learners understand exactly how their work is judged.

What I Built

A self-paced IELTS Writing Task 2 course on MoodleCloud with:

  • → 4 units covering Essay Structure, Supporting Ideas, and Assessment.
  • → 4-level rubric mapped directly to official IELTS Band Descriptors.
  • → Activity gating — sequential mastery enforced by Moodle's completion tracking.
Structured Curriculum: Logic-gated units ensuring a linear learning journey.
Progress Tracking

Centralized Gradebook gives learners and teachers a clear view of unit-by-unit progress.

Linear Pathway

Unit gating ensures learners master foundational concepts before moving to complex practice.

Design Decisions

Direct Mapping

Simplifying criteria creates a gap. Mapping directly means learners practice with official standards.

Sequential Mastery

Gating prevents skipping, ensuring instructional rigor across the entire course.

What I Learned

  • → Moodle's rubric tool requires accessible yet accurate translation of official criteria.
  • → LMS structure can guide behavior automatically, reducing manual intervention.
