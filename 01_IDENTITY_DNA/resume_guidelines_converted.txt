Resume Guidelines
1. Keep it to 1–3 pages 1 page if you have <5 years of experience. Cut what doesn’t help you get this role.
2. Start with a sharp professional summary Highlight your years of experience, industry, key skills, and impact.
3. Tailor your Resume to the job description Mirror 3–5 keywords from the JD. Aim for at least a 70% match.
4. Focus on achievements, not responsibilities Use results to show what changed because of you — not just what you were assigned.
5. Use action verbs to start each bullet (check next page) E.g., Led, Improved, Launched, Streamlined, Delivered.
6. Quantify wherever possible Show scale, scope, or results (e.g., “Reduced churn by 18%” or “Managed $200K budget”).
7. Highlight recent and relevant experience first Condense or cut older or unrelated roles. Prioritize what supports this application.
8. Show career progression Include promotions, added responsibilities, or team growth to signal momentum.
9. Use industry-standard job titles If your past titles are confusing, adjust them to match market terms.
10. List relevant tools, tech, or certifications Keep it focused — don’t list every course you’ve ever taken.
11. Avoid jargon Translate company-specific terms into language the recruiter will understand.
12. Use a professional email + LinkedIn E.g., firstname.lastname2211@gmail.com. No nicknames. Add your LinkedIn profile link.
13. Proofread carefully Typos = instant red flag. Use tools like Grammarly or get a second pair of eyes.
14. Don’t include irrelevant info Skip hobbies, full address, references, or anything that wastes space.
15. Save as Resume_FirstName_LastName and send as PDF Word files can break formatting when opened with another OS (e.g. Windows to Mac). PDFs are safe.
