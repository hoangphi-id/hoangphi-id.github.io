# 🛡️ CASE STUDY: THE DEADLINE CRISIS SIMULATOR
**Role:** Full-Stack Learning Architect
**Tools:** Storyline 360, JavaScript, Google Apps Script, Rise 360
**Challenge:** Bridging the gap between linguistic knowledge and professional remote communication under pressure.

---

## 🚀 1. SCENARIO DESIGN RATIONALE (Lý do thiết kế)

### The Problem
Professional non-native English speakers often master grammar but fail at **Tone and Timing** in high-stakes remote environments. Standard e-learning provides passive multiple-choice questions with no real consequences, leading to a disconnect between training and real-world behavior.

### The Solution: "The Deadline Crisis"
I designed a branching simulation that mirrors a realistic Monday morning crisis: a delayed project, a stressed manager, and a demanding client. 
- **Non-Linear Feedback:** Instead of "Correct/Incorrect," learners experience the **Consequences** of their words.
- **The Dual-Metric System:** Every decision impacts two hidden variables:
    1. **Trust Score (Social Capital):** Long-term relationship health with coworkers.
    2. **Stress Level (Operational Capacity):** The immediate pressure on the project timeline.

---

## 🏗️ 2. TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE (Hệ thống kỹ thuật)

### Branching Logic (The "Neural Network")
This simulator features a complex branching structure with **[X] main decision points** and **3 distinct endings**:
1. **Mastery:** High Trust, Low Stress (The project is saved, and relationships are strengthened).
2. **Survival:** High Stress, Neutral Trust (The project is done, but the team is burnt out).
3. **Crisis:** Low Trust, High Stress (The project fails, and professional reputation is damaged).
